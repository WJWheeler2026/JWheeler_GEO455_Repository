The North Carolina Solar Atlas

An interactive map showcasing solar infrastructure, radiation potential, and optimal environmental conditions across North Carolina.

Launch Viewer ⬇

About This Map

I created this web map with the goal of combining environmental and infrastructure data to help users explore the possibility of expanding solar potential in the state of North Carolina.

North Carolina consistently ranks in the top 5 states nationwide for solar energy production, making it an excellent choice for exploring future possibilities for solar farming in the state. In fact, in 2024-2025, NC was ranked 4th in the country for solar farming, with around 7.5-10% of all power coming from solar.

Within the North Carolina Solar Atlas, users will interact with up-to-date data regarding existing projects, solar resources, and terrain, all of which are meant to help users determine where in the state is best for solar.

Built using Leaflet, ArcGIS Pro and PHCode | GIS Data from United States Geological Survey, National Laboraory of the Rockies Renewable Energy Atlas, and NC OneMap

Methods

All data required extensive cleaning, aside from the terrain data. All datasets were reprojected in order to keep things consistent, and Clip functions were completed to ensure all data fit the bounds of North Carolina only. NC OneMap terrain data collected in the form of a hillshade was run through the "Slope" and "Aspect" tools in ArcGIS Pro, from which they were both reclassified into having three classes each: Ideal, Acceptable, Avoid. Vector data were converted to GeoJSONs, whereas raster data was first converted to vector data before being fecthed as GeoJSONs.
